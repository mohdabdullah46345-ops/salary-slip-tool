How to Use the Salary Slip Generator

Last updated: 9 June 2026 · 5 min read

A salary slip (also called a payslip or pay stub) is a document that an employer gives to an employee every pay period. It lists how much the employee earned, what was deducted, and the final take-home (net) pay. Our free Salary Slip Generator lets you create a clean, professional payslip in minutes — directly in your browser, without installing anything. This guide walks you through every step.

Step 1: Enter Your Company Details

Start in the Company Details section on the left. Add your company name, address, and a phone number or email. You can also click the + Logo box to upload your company logo (PNG or JPG under 1 MB). The logo and company name will appear at the top of the payslip, giving it a branded, official look.

Step 2: Set the Pay Period and Attendance

In the Pay Period & Attendance section, pick the salary month, then enter the total number of working days and any "Loss of Pay" (LOP) days. The tool automatically calculates the number of paid days for you. This is useful when an employee took unpaid leave during the month.

Step 3: Fill in Employee Details

Under Employee Details, enter the employee's name, ID, designation, department, date of joining, PAN, bank name, account number, and UAN/PF number. You can also change the currency symbol (the default is the Indian Rupee, ₹). Leave any field blank if it is not relevant — it will simply show a dash on the payslip.

Step 4: Add Earnings

In the Earnings section you will find common rows already added for you:

  • Basic Salary — the fixed core component of the salary.
  • House Rent Allowance (HRA) — an allowance towards rent.
  • Conveyance Allowance — towards travel/commute costs.
  • Special Allowance — any remaining/balancing component.

You can edit the label and amount of any row, remove rows you don't need, or click + Add Earning to include extra components like Medical Allowance, Bonus, or Overtime. The tool adds everything up into Gross Earnings automatically.

Step 5: Add Deductions

The Deductions section works the same way. Typical deductions include:

  • Provident Fund (PF) — a retirement savings contribution.
  • Professional Tax — a small state-level tax (where applicable).
  • TDS — income tax deducted at source.

Add or remove deductions as needed. The tool totals them as Total Deductions.

Step 6: Review the Net Pay

The payslip automatically calculates Net Pay = Gross Earnings − Total Deductions and even writes the amount in words using the Indian numbering system (e.g. "Sixty Four Thousand Nine Hundred Rupees Only"). Everything in the preview on the right updates instantly as you type.

Step 7: Download as PDF

When you are happy with the preview, click the ⬇ Download PDF button. Your browser's print dialog will open — choose "Save as PDF" as the destination and save. The exported PDF contains only the payslip (the form and buttons are hidden), so you get a clean, professional A4 document ready to share or print.

Helpful Tips

  • Auto-save: Your details are saved in your browser, so you can come back later and continue.

  • Reset: Click "Reset" to clear everything and start fresh.
